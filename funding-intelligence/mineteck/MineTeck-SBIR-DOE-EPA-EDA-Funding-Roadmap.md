## 1. Executive Summary

MineTeck may qualify for multiple U.S. funding pathways if its assay reports, laboratory analysis, feedstock data, and recovery process can demonstrate a repeatable, innovative method for recovering precious and strategic metals from overlooked waste streams.

The strongest funding narrative is not simply that MineTeck recovers gold, silver, copper, or palladium. The stronger positioning is:

> MineTeck is developing a technology-enabled urban mining and critical materials recovery platform that transforms overlooked waste streams into domestic sources of precious and strategic metals. Through advanced feedstock characterization, recovery optimization, and resource intelligence, MineTeck supports North American supply-chain resilience, circular economy objectives, and critical minerals security.

This places MineTeck in a stronger category for DOE SBIR/STTR, NSF SBIR, EPA circular economy programs, DoD strategic materials programs, EDA industrial development funding, state economic development incentives, tribal economic development partnerships, and strategic investor / infrastructure capital formation.

## 2. Core Claim To Validate

Current process is reported to obtain:

- Approximately **8 oz per ton of gold**
- Silver recovery potential
- Copper recovery potential
- Palladium recovery potential

The gold claim alone, if verified, converts roughly to:

- 8 troy oz/ton x 31.1035 grams = approximately **248.8 grams per ton**

This is exceptionally high-grade material compared with conventional mining benchmarks. However, fundability depends on verified evidence, repeatable recovery, and a clear technology innovation.

### Required Evidence

MineTeck should assemble:

1. Independent assay reports
2. Laboratory analysis
3. Recovery test results
4. Metallurgical reports
5. Sampling methodology
6. Chain-of-custody documentation
7. Feedstock source description
8. Recovery percentage data
9. Process economics
10. Annual feedstock volume estimates

## 3. SBIR Eligibility Logic

SBIR does **not** fund ordinary mining operations or commodity extraction. It funds research and development with commercialization potential.

### Not Strong SBIR Positioning

> We recover gold from material.

### Strong SBIR Positioning

> We have developed a novel technology-enabled process to identify, characterize, and recover critical and precious metals from overlooked urban mining feedstocks that are currently underutilized, landfilled, or economically misclassified.

### Possible Innovation Areas

- AI feedstock identification
- AI resource valuation
- Novel sorting or separation process
- Novel recovery chemistry
- Low-energy extraction method
- Recovery from previously uneconomic material
- Carbon impact quantification
- Circular economy intelligence platform
- Domestic critical mineral supply-chain resilience

## 6. Capital Stack Strategy

MineTeck should not rely on a single grant pathway. The stronger approach is a layered capital stack.

```text
Assays + Lab Validation
   ↓
Proof of Process
   ↓
SBIR Phase I
   ↓
SBIR Phase II
   ↓
Pilot Facility
   ↓
DOE / EDA / State Infrastructure Funding
   ↓
Strategic Industry Partner
   ↓
Private Capital / Infrastructure Capital
```

### Suggested Funding Stack

| Layer | Purpose |
|---|---|
| SBIR Phase I | Technical feasibility |
| SBIR Phase II | Prototype / pilot development |
| DOE Programs | Critical materials and demonstration funding |
| EPA Programs | Circular economy and environmental validation |
| EDA Programs | Industrial facility and regional job creation |

| Tribal Partnerships | Economic development, environmental stewardship, regional project support |
