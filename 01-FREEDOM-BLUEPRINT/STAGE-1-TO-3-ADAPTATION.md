# Freedom Blueprint Stage 1 to Stage 3 Adaptation

## Stage 1 — From Zero to $10k/month

### Objective

Build practical AI-powered business tools, sell them to real businesses, and convert one-time builds into recurring support.

### Core Deliverables

- Lead capture and qualification system
- Booking and appointment system
- AI chatbot or intake assistant
- Client dashboard
- Document automation workflow

### Revenue Model

```text
Setup fee: $3,000 to $7,500
Monthly support: $500 to $1,500
```

### Stage 1 Weekly Actions

- Build one demo product
- Create a list of 25 prospects
- Personalize 5 demos per week
- Send direct outreach
- Close first client even if pricing is modest
- Turn every delivery into a testimonial and retainer

### Exit Criteria

- 5+ paying clients
- 3+ monthly retainers
- 1 repeatable offer with documented delivery steps
- At least one public/private case study

---

## Stage 2 — From $10k to $30k/month

### Objective

Turn custom work into a productized delivery machine.

### Core Moves

1. Productize the best-selling offer
2. Create delivery templates
3. Build reusable code starters
4. Delegate lead research and admin
5. Launch referral system
6. Publish authority content around builds

### Productized Offer Standard

Each productized offer must have:

- Fixed problem
- Fixed buyer
- Fixed scope
- Fixed timeline
- Fixed setup price
- Fixed monthly support option
- Clear before/after outcome

### Exit Criteria

- One primary offer responsible for most revenue
- Delivery time reduced by 50%+
- 10+ active client relationships
- Outreach or referral engine working weekly
- Founder no longer doing all admin work

---

## Stage 3 — From Services to SaaS

### Objective

Turn repeated client problems into software products.

### The Founding Partner Pattern

Find one client with a painful recurring problem. Offer to build the MVP with them as the first user.

Recommended structure:

- Client funds part of MVP build
- Client receives founding-member pricing
- You retain IP
- Client receives license and priority input
- Their use case becomes the first case study

### SaaS Candidate Criteria

A service becomes SaaS-worthy when:

- 3+ clients have the same problem
- The workflow is repeated weekly or daily
- The buyer already pays for a manual workaround
- Data, dashboard, automation, or communication are central
- The value increases over time as data accumulates

### Exit Criteria

- 1 founding partner signed
- MVP launched
- First 5-10 SaaS users acquired
- Service business still funds operations
- SaaS revenue trend is visible and repeatable
