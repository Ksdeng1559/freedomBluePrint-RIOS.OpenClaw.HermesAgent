# Founding Partner Playbook

## Purpose

Convert a repeated client problem into a funded SaaS MVP without building in isolation.

## When to Use This

Use this only when:

- 3+ clients have the same recurring problem
- The problem is expensive or urgent
- The workflow can be standardized
- The buyer would pay monthly for a better system
- One client is collaborative enough to shape the MVP

## Founding Partner Offer

```text
I have an idea I want to bring to you.

I have noticed that [problem] keeps showing up in your business and in similar businesses.

Instead of building this as a one-off custom tool, I want to build it as a software product with you as the first founding partner.

Here is the structure:

1. You fund part of the MVP build at a reduced custom-build cost.
2. You get founding-member pricing for long-term access.
3. Your workflow shapes the first version.
4. I retain the IP and can sell the product to other businesses.
5. You get priority support and influence on the roadmap.

This gives you the tool you need faster and at a lower cost than a fully custom build, while giving me a real-world case study and product foundation.

Would it be worth mapping this out together?
```

## Qualification Criteria

A founding partner must have:

- Exact problem fit
- Budget
- Urgency
- Collaborative mindset
- Willingness to provide feedback
- Ability to become a case study

## SaaS Conversion Worksheet

### Problem

What repeated problem is being solved?

### Buyer

Who pays for the solution?

### User

Who uses it daily or weekly?

### Current workaround

How is the problem handled today?

### Value

What does fixing it save or generate?

### MVP

What is the smallest product that solves the core workflow?

### Pricing

What monthly price is justified?

### Expansion

What features increase retention or account value?

## Ownership Rule

The operator keeps IP by default.

The client receives a license, preferential pricing, and influence — not ownership — unless a separate equity or joint venture agreement is intentionally negotiated.

## SaaS Launch Sequence

```text
1. Identify repeated problem
2. Draft SaaS PRD
3. Pitch founding partner
4. Sign agreement
5. Build MVP
6. Deploy to partner
7. Collect feedback weekly
8. Create case study
9. Add first 5-10 customers
10. Decide whether to scale, pause, or fold back into service offer
```
